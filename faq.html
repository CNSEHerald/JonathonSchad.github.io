How do I get involved with CNSE UGSA?

By being a full-time student at CNSE you are already a member of the UGSA. All UGSA members can run for and be elected to one of two class representative positions that are filled every year. If running for office isn't for you, UGSA is always looking for volunteers to serve on committees under the Executive Board or even College Assembly or Governance Council committees. If you like planning events, try the UGSA Events Committee. Other committees plan what classes will be taught, design student spaces on campus, and plan outreach activities.

What events does UGSA have each year?

UGSA holds an annual welcome week which includes Freshman Orientation activities as well as a block party and club social. At the block party, students can enjoy great food, music, and games. The club social is a great way for new and returning students to learn about what clubs are operating on campus and how to get involved. Later in the Fall semester, UGSA helps host the Annual CNSE Paintball Competition for students, faculty, and adminstration. In the spring semester, UGSA hosts a video game tournament as well as a lasertag competition.

How can I contact my UGSA representative?

Feel free to send your concerns through the Contact Us page on the website. Executive board members' emails are available on the Executive Board page. The Executive board holds bi-weekly meeting that has an open forum section. Check the UGSA calendar for when the next bi-weekly meeting will take place. Currently UGSA doesn't have an official office to stop by at and address your concerns in person but our Executive board is always available to talk between classes.

Is UGSA the official Student Association for SUNY Poly students on the Albany campus

Yes and no. UGSA is the only organization currently advocating for SUNY Poly students on the CNSE campus. Due to the larger size and longer standing of the former SUNYIT Student Association, they currently collect all Student Activity fees from SUNY Poly students. The fees from CNSE students are then given to the UGSA to disperse to student organizations on the CNSE campus. Progress is being made to officially recognize both student associations.

Does UGSA represent UAlbany students that attend CNSE?

Yes. All student organizations, committees, and elections are open to UAlbany students during the transition period.
