Top Story: The Changing Face of Grief

by Erin Rousseau

30 MARCH 2016

The age of communication has caused gaps in many aspects of our society. Unlike the millennia that came before, the Internet has forced mourning to become an inherently public and personal act. Moreover, the connectivity of the 21st century has caused changes in the way we look at grief and mourning through communication by social media and applied technologies.

Zika Linked to Guillain-Barré Syndrome

by Leonardo Bezerra

11 MARCH 2016

The Zika virus is an infectious virus that is known to be transmitted by mosquitoes, specifically the species Aedes aegypti, and is also believed to be sexually transmissible. Researchers from the Institute of Emerging Diseases at Polynesia have discovered a link between Zika and another poorly understood disease, Guillain-Barré syndrome.

Female Libido Drug Fails to Perform

by Audrey Crom

8 MARCH 2016

In August 2015, the US Food and Drug Administration approved the use of flibanserin as a drug treatment for women who experience low libido, otherwise known as hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD). Recent studies, however, have reported an increase of about only one half of one sexual encounter per month for women who have taken the drug during clinical trials.

About The Herald

The CNSE Herald is a student-run electronic news source serving SUNY Polytechnic Institute. Operating out of the desk next to NFE 4423, the Herald covers topics pertaining to student interests ranging from local and world news to the latest updates in science. The electronic publishing platform allows students to gain experience with technical and journalistic writing, photography, and web design while staying current with the demand for online media.
