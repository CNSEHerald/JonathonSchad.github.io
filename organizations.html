The CNSE Herald

Jacqueline Elwood, Editor-in-Chief, thecnseherald@gmail.com

The CNSE Herald is a student-run electronic news source serving SUNY Polytechnic Institute. Operating out of the desk next to NFE 4423, the Herald covers topics pertaining to student interests ranging from local and world news to the latest updates in science. The electronic publishing platform allows students to gain experience with technical and journalistic writing, photography, and web design while staying current with the demand for online media.

Music Club

Students from the music club have performed at graduation, the holiday party, and at a CNSE-hosted open-mic talent show.

Nanoelectronic Club

Mikhail Yermakov, myermakov@sunypoly.edu

NanoElectronics Club (NEC) is a newly established student organization at CNSE. Our primary objective is the academic success of the members, particularly in the courses related to Electrical Engineering, Electronics and VLSI. Our key ideas are relevance to the coursework and student support. We prioritize a mathematical approach to engineering and bottom-up design ”from scratch” rather than modular assembly: foundation comes first, innovation follows. We work to develop a thorough theoretical basis which goes along with the material taught at CNSE and enhances students’ experience by means of study groups and tutoring sessions, as well as hands-on projects.

Rock Climbing Club

Spencer Natman, snatman@albany.edu

The CNSE Rock Climbing club is nothing short of adventure. Our activities are comprised of bouldering, toproping, lead climbing, amongst other disciplines of rock climbing. The experienced and the inexperienced are all welcome to participate in our regular trips to The Edge in Halfmoon. The inexperienced will learn all they need to know, with practice, to safely climb, and the experienced will be able to quickly jump into a casual, satisfying climbing environment. We, as a club, aspire to take outdoor climbing trips to expand on this already delightful and rewarding activity.

4STEM

Wesley Sherow, wsherow@sunypoly.edu

4STEM is the first dedicated student-run volunteering club at CNSE. It focuses on volunteering time and services for the purpose of improving enrollment and success of students in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) related education. 4STEM is also incorporated as an official non-profit for the purpose of making the biggest change possible in the world. All volunteering done under 4STEM is recorded and can be listed on your resume.

AVS

Nick Pieniazek, npieniazek@albany.edu

The purpose of the American Vacuum Society CNSE Chapter is threefold. The first being to give students as much exposure to the professional environment in a variety of ways which includes but is not limited to career fairs, resume critiquing sessions, internship application walk throughs and networking advisement. Our second mission goal is to encourage critical thinking development skills. Critical thinking skills are not something someone is simply born with. It is a skill that is practice honed and never perfected. AVS aims to test and develop these skills by encouraging students to engage in activities such as Philosophy Night. Our final mission statement and quite possibly our most beloved is to just relax, have fun and take some time to recoup the mind. Highly intense schools such as CNSE can often take the pizzazz out of life and we at AVS try to maximize comradery by letting students have fun in group activities like bridge building competitions, open mic nights and trivia contests. When these objectives are combined it makes for a club that provides both long term professional development as well as lifelong critical thinking skills that will only enrich the lives of AVS participants.

UAV Club

Miguel Ramirez, mramirez@albany.edu

UAV Club brings students together interested in designing, building and flying drones. Students are free to suggest ideas they would like to try regarding drones or projects. Members who are interested in learning how to fly are able to do so by flying already built planes. We have participated in the global space balloon challenge and received funding from SUNY Polytechnic Institute to do so. In addition, members have gotten together to create a quad-copter obstacle course for first-person view quad-copters. We also attend aerospace museums whenever we have the chance to do so as well as participate in outreach events to teach younger students about drones.

Tri-Beta

Amanda August, aaugust@albany.edu

Beta Beta Beta (TriBeta) is a society for undergraduate and graduate students dedicated to improving the understanding and appreciation of biological study and extending boundaries of human knowledge through scientific research. Since its founding in 1922, more than 200,000 persons have been accepted into lifetime membership, and more than 670 chapters have been established throughout the United States and Puerto Rico. As an interest club, we are looking for second semester sophomores or older to join us in starting the honor society. Must be on the track for a biological science concentration or major.
