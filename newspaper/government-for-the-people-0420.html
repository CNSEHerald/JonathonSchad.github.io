Government for the People?

 

 

By Leonardo Bezerra, Science & Technology Editor | 20 April 2016

Opinion

animalwall.xyz

This year has seen one of the most interesting presidential campaign runs in history. The bombastic Donald Trump continues to lead polls for the Republican Party. Democratic Sen. Bernie Sanders has come up from a 70% poll deficit under Secretary Hilary Clinton, winning 8 of the last 10 state caucuses. Meanwhile, the Establishment has pushed against these wild cards to get them to lose the race. Are Americans really in charge of selecting their leaders anymore?

 

In Colorado, the Republican primary was effectively canceled by the Republican executive board, giving Ted Cruz all of the state’s delegates, regardless of the vote. There are even stronger claims of Cruz’s corruption by Trump. What is known is that while it is illegal to buy votes from both federal and state laws, there is little on the books on the legality of “buying” delegates, whom may vote freely for any candidate. Trump erupted at this injustice on Fox news.

 

“I’ve gotten millions … of more votes than [Sen. Ted] Cruz, and I’ve gotten hundreds of delegates more, and we keep fighting, fighting, fighting, and then you have a Colorado where they just get all of these delegates, and it’s not [even] a system,” Trump said, during the Fox News broadcast. “There was no voting. I didn’t go out there to make a speech or anything. There’s no voting.”

 

All 37 of Colorado’s delegates were given to Ted Cruz, regardless of the poll. According to The Denver Post, the Republican executive committee "voted to cancel the traditional presidential preference poll after the national party changed its rules to require a state's delegates to support the candidate that wins the caucus vote."

 

 Meanwhile in Wyoming, a vote was held was held for the Democratic leaders. Sanders won by a clear 10 percent of the vote, of 55 to 45. Yet the delegates awarded to each candidate were the same. But Wyoming only has 14 delegates, so that was the best they could split it, you may say.

 

The public can see a clear bias towards Clinton in other states as well. In Colorado, Sanders won the state by a large margin at 59.0 to 40.3 percent. The Democratic Party then accidently lost ten precinct’s voter data. This mistake went unreported until a reporter at the Denver Post noticed the blip and alerted the party leaders. One delegate was stripped from Clinton and given to Sanders, resolving the discrepancy. Afterward, the mistake was only reported the Clinton campaign, while the Sanders campaign was unaware until this reporter alerted them to the issue.

 

 This is one of the most heated presidential primaries in recent history. The Establishment is fighting to maintain power for both sides of the table from newcomers who may disrupt the status quo. But we should stay alert that the results from the primary are indeed from the votes of the people, not the decree of those in power.
