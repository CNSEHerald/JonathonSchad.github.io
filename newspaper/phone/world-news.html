Radovan Karadzic's Trial: Shining Hope for Human Rights or Another Example of Incompetence?

On March 24th, former president of Republika Srpska, Radovan Karadzic, was found guilty of genocide, war crimes, and crimes against humanity.  The U.N.-established International Criminal Tribunal for the Former Yugoslavia (ICTY) sentenced 70-year-old Karadzic to 40 years in prison.  This sentence comes almost 21 years after he was indicted for genocide and crimes against humanity in July of 1995.
