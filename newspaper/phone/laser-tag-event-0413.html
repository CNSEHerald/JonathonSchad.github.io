Student Activities Hosts Laser Tag Event for SUNY Poly Students

 

By Deanna Soucie | 13 April 2016

Jonathon Schad

The laser tag event at Zero Gravity Laser Tag was a huge success for the SUNY Poly Albany site Student Life in a multitude of ways; it was a great way to encourage and promote the mingling of different classes and reached an impressively large number of SUNY Poly students.

 

The outing consisted of two hours of laser tag in a series of fifteen-minute games, in which students took shots at each other in a neon room filled of obstacles.  Between which students discussed strategy, compared scores, and played in the small arcade room. 

Many of the students there where people whom I barely recognized.  Although they were all my peers in this small college of ours, I had not met many of the students who attended this event, yet now I have fought next to them on a neon-labyrinth field of battle.  UGSA Vice President and senior class representative, Jeff Richards, "thought it was very successful and allowed for students of all classes to mingle with each other in a team building event."  He was also pleased with how well it allowed for communication between classes at SUNY Poly, something he thought was "important for any school to encourage."  SUNY Poly students of all ages where working together to defend their territory, cover each other in times of heavy laser fire, and storm the heavily defended bridge.  This event was an excellent choice to allow students the chance to work together as a team and get rid of some stress in the process.  

In addition to promoting camaraderie between the different undergraduate classes, this event was also a success in the number of students it reached. Around 30 SUNY Poly undergraduates attended the event, which Richards believes is great compared to turnouts at past student focused programs. Events like laser tag, which appeal to large numbers of students, show that student life at SUNY Poly Albany Site is improving.  According to Richards, many of the freshmen that attended laser tag had a blast, and I did as well.

Due to the success of SUNY Poly laser tag, it's likely that we may see other similar events in the future.  Events which, if are anything like this one, I would strongly recommend you attend.

Ana Segarceanu
