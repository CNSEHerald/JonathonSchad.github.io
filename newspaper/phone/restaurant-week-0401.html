Food Truck Festival and Restaurant Week Coming to Albany this April

 

By Audrey Crom | 1 April 2016

Dribbble/icon

Attention all food connoisseurs or anyone who appreciates a good deal on some great food!  Mark your calendars for the second week of April and prepare those stomachs for the 12th annual Downtown Albany Restaurant Week.  The week will kick off strong on Friday, April 8th with the food truck event, Fork in the Road, being held in Tercentennial Park.  From there, the irresistible week-long deal of a full three course meal for only $20.16 at any of 17 participating restaurants will commence on April 9th and will continue through April 15th.

 

Fork in the Road is hosted and organized by the Downtown Albany BID (Business Improvement District) and will be held on Friday April 8th from 5:00pm to 8:00pm in Tricentennial Park in downtown Albany, located on Broadway between Stueben and Columbia Street.  Admission is free and open to anyone. Several of the best food trucks and vendors that Albany has to offer will be showcased all in one convenient location. Live music will also be provided by a local band, The Revival. After the event on April 8th, Fork in the Road will be held once a month until October. These dates include: May 13th, June 10th, July 8th, August 12th, September 9th, and October 14th.  The BID is currently looking for feedback and is open to suggestion on which food trucks and vendors people would like to see in attendance for any subsequent Fork in the Road events.

 

The real fun begins on Saturday April 9th with Restaurant Week. During this week-long period, 17 restaurants will be offering full three course meals for a flat rate of $20.16. The only stipulation is that customers are restricted to a limited menu, which is stated to have a normal retail minimum of $35.00. So, you are saving at least $15 right off the bat. Tax, beverages, and gratuity are not included in the $20.16 price, however, and customers are urged to base gratuity on the full, original value of the meal and the services rendered.

 

The 17 participating restaurants include the following:

 

  • Aji Steak Stone & Sushi
  • Ama Cocina
  • Café Capriccio
  • The Capital Lounge
  • C.H.  Evans Brewing at the Albany Pump Station
  • Charter at the Hilton
  • City Beer Hall
  • Dawn’s Victory Sports Café
  • The Hollow Bar + Kitchen
  • Hudson Harbor Streak and Seafood
  • Jack’s Oyster House
  • La Serre
  • The Merry Monk
  • Olde English Pub and Pantry
  • Parish Public House
  • Public House 42
  • V&R Italian Ristorante

 

Most of these restaurants are on the higher end of the spectrum and would otherwise be out of the normal budget range for the average college student.  This week-long deal provides the perfect opportunity for budgeted students to seize the opportunity to try these quality eateries at a fraction of the price.  Reservations are strongly recommended, not only because it might be near impossible to score a table by walking in, but also because most locations require a reservation be made in order to cash in on the deal. Before making plans for a certain restaurant, it is always wise to contact the location ahead of time for individual specifications.

 

The deals become even better for students on Student Night, which takes place on Monday April 11th. The $20.16 base price is reduced down to an even more affordable $16.16 at a select number of participating restaurants limited to Aji Steak Stone & Sushi, The Hollow Bar + Kitchen, Jack’s Oyster House, La Serre, Olde English Pub and Pantry, and V&R Italian Ristorante.  Be sure to bring a valid student ID and make reservations before it’s too late to secure a coveted spot at these locations amongst the frenzy of hungry college students.

 

For those with cars who want to circumvent the bus system and brave the jungle that is downtown Albany parking, free on-street parking will be offered from 5:00pm to 8:00pm Monday through Friday and all day on Saturday and Sunday during the duration of Restaurant week. Several parking garages, including Riverfront Parking Garage and Quackenbush & Green-Hudson Garages are also offering free parking.

 

 Anyone who wants to experience the best cuisine that downtown Albany has to offer should take full advantage of all Restaurant Week has to offer without having to worry too much about breaking the bank.  Your stomach will thank you for it.
