FROM THE EDITOR: Introducing the CNSE Herald

 

By Jacqueline Elwood, Editor-in-Chief | 1 March 2016

This week, we at The Herald have begun our mission to bring news in a completely transparent manner written by the students, for the students. With the rise of digital journalism, we want to stay up-to-date with the latest forms of media consumption.

 

The advent of sponsored content, click-bait, and other search engine optimization tools, has caused the field of journalism as we know it to distort. As evidenced by the shut-down of Al Jazeera America. We at The Herald hope to embody traditional journalism. We hope to show what unsponsored content looks like and foster an interest in reading or watching unbiased news sources. Perhaps it's just a pipe dream to expect so much out of a tiny, student newspaper, but that won't stop us from trying.

 

This has not been an independent effort and there are many people that have contributed to the success of this organization. Jonathan Schad '16, President of the Undergraduate Student Association, has spearheaded the effort to secure a website for The Herald and implement the initial design. Christopher Kendra '17, Treasurer of the Undergraduate Student Association, has been key in providing the budget for The Herald to get off the ground. All members of the Undergraduate Student Association have been helpful in the fight for a space for The Herald to operate within the College of Nanoscale Science and Engineering.

 

Feel free to send any questions or feedback to thecnseherald@gmail.com. As an upstart organization, we are seeking new, talented writers, photographers, reviewers, and those interested in front- and back-end web design. I encourage all interested students to attend one of our general interest meetings this semester.

 

Your Editor-in-Chief,

 

Jacqueline Elwood
