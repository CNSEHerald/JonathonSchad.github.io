Professor's Spotlight

Dr. Susan Sharfstein

To many younger SUNY Poly students, Dr. Susan Sharfstein is the Chemistry professor.  Teaching introductory chemistry has a way of ‘typecasting’ a professor to younger students.

 

As students continue at CNSE, they will see more of Professor Sharfstein.  If a student is in the engineering track, they will also have her for ‘Fluid Mechanics and Transport Processes’, an extremely engineering heavy course.  Students who take the more biology geared classes will find Professor Sharfstein giving guest lectures on topics ranging from biologic batteries to biomanufacturing.

 

So what type of background must Professor Sharfstein have in order to teach chemistry, fluid dynamics, and all manner of biology?

FDA Approves First 3D Printed Drug

In August of 2015, for the first time, the FDA approved a 3D-printed drug called Spirtam.  As of March 22, 2016, the drug is now available in the US.  Spritam, produced by Aprecia Pharmaceuticals, is a prescription pill that will aid in the treatment of various forms of epilepsy, a condition that affects around 3 million Americans.  Perhaps equally important, it is a drug created through a process that may set a precedent revolutionizing the pharmaceutical industry.

The Great Barrier Reef Turns White

Tourists traveling to Australia to see the Great Barrier Reef may be surprised by the white coral and the absence of fish covering the sea floor.  No longer colorful, the coral populations are experiencing a rise in bleaching.

Internet Names National Environmental Research Vessel 'Boaty McBoatface'

The National Environmental Research Council turned to the Internet for suggestions on a name for the UK’s new polar research vessel. The Internet cast their votes, giving the ‘RSS Boaty McBoatface’ a commanding lead over the rest of the competition.

The Changing Face of Grief

The age of communication has caused gaps in many aspects of our society. Unlike the millennia that came before, the Internet has forced mourning to become an inherently public and personal act. Moreover, the connectivity of the 21st century has caused changes in the way we look at grief and mourning through communication by social media and applied technologies.

Zika Linked to Guillain-Barré Syndrome

The Zika virus has mostly been considered a tropical disease, like dengue. Due to global warming, its area of influence is spreading to higher latitudes, making millions of people at risk. Researchers from the Institute of Emerging Diseases at Polynesia have discovered a link between Zika and another poorly understood disease, Guillain-Barré syndrome.
