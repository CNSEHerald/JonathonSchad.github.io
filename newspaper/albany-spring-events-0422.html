Spring into Albany

 

 

By Deanna Soucie | 22 April 2016

Annual Festivals: Albany Tulip Festival

 

Tulips are carefully planted in detailed patterns to produce amazing shapes and designs like those seen here from the 2011 Tulip Festival.

albanyweblog.com

 

Saturday, May 7 and Sunday, May 8: The tulips, vendors, food, music, and more are on display to the public from 11 AM to 6 PM and admission is free.

 

Tulip Festival is the pinnacle of Albany spring events and will be held this May for the 68th time.  This event is held every year on Mother’s Day weekend in Downtown Albany’s Washington Park.  Featuring hundreds of homemade craft vendors, food, live music, a fine arts show, and over 140,000 individual tulips of more than 150 varieties, this festival is a truly dazzling event that should be attended at least once by everyone and anyone who calls Albany home.

 

Sponsored by local alternative radio station, WEQX, The Main Stage performances promise to delight. On Saturday, The Parlor opens at 1:30PM before Tokyo Police Club takes the stage at 3:00PM. Headliners, Langhorne Slim & The Law are set to begin at 4:30PM. The 518 Stage will showcase local talent on both days of the festival, which will make for a uniquely upstate listening experience.

 

Music: Alive at Five

 

Flickr

 

Every Thursday evening from 5-8 PM between June 9 and August 4: Located at the amphitheater (Jennings Landing) in the Corning Preserve in Downtown Albany.  Admission is free.

 

Starting June 9th, Albany’s annual free concert series Alive at Five kicks off and runs every Thursday, starting at five o’clock, until early august.  If you plan on being in the area over the break, Alive at Five can be a fun way to spend an evening.  This concert series offers a diverse array of musical styles performed by regional and national artists and numerous vendors off-stage.  This fun concert series was even voted “The Capital Region’s Best Concert Series” by Metroland.

 

Past acts have included up-and-coming talent in addition to established names. Last year alt-rock group, Atlas Genius, delighted the 518. The Blue Oyster Cult ended the summer series with their unique blend of intelligent hard rock. This year’s summer line up has yet to be announced.

Science- Alton Brown Live: Eat Your Science (at Proctors)

 

Kalamazoo Section of the American Chemical Society

 

April 30 at 8 PM: Located at Proctors Theatre in Schenectady, Tickets range from $40 to $135.

 

This science themed show is a hodgepodge of comedy, music, food, puppets, science, and the occasional “potentially dangerous experiment”.  There is strong audience participation, which Alton himself even believes has the potential to be spark “therapy inducing opportunities”.  A follow-up to Alton’s “Edible Inevitable” tour, “Eat Your Science” is highly rated and sure to impress, amaze, and, of course, educate

Entertainment: Spin Jam

 

Deanna Soucie

 

Every Tuesday evening at 8 PM:  Located at the amphitheater (Jennings Landing) in the Corning Preserve in Downtown Albany.  Admission is free.

 

If you’re looking for something out of the ordinary, Spin Jam might be the Albany Spring event for you.  This unusual gathering features many hobby fire poi performers showing off their various spinning skills.  Flaming swords, poi, and hula-hoops are usually seen, flanked by non-flaming hula-hoopers, jugglers, a drum circle, the occasional unicycle, and sometimes a tie-dye t-shirt vendor.   On evenings with fair weather, this little gathering can almost look like a circus and always makes for an exciting escape from the mundane.
