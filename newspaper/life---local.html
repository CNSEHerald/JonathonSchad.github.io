Professor's Spotlight

Dr. Susan Sharfstein

To many younger SUNY Poly students, Dr. Susan Sharfstein is the Chemistry professor.  Teaching introductory chemistry has a way of ‘typecasting’ a professor to younger students.

 

As students continue at CNSE, they will see more of Professor Sharfstein.  If a student is in the engineering track, they will also have her for ‘Fluid Mechanics and Transport Processes’, an extremely engineering heavy course.  Students who take the more biology geared classes will find Professor Sharfstein giving guest lectures on topics ranging from biologic batteries to biomanufacturing.

 

So what type of background must Professor Sharfstein have in order to teach chemistry, fluid dynamics, and all manner of biology?

Spring into Albany

A quick look at the exciting events happening around Albany this spring and early summer.

NY Legislature Enacts SUNY-wide In-state Tuition Freeze

This past week, the New York State Legislature and Governor Andrew Cuomo finalized the New York state budget and failed to renew the NY SUNY 2020 legislation, which had previously allowed for a steady, predictable increase in SUNY tuition.

New Student Space Creates Mixed Responses

The long awaited SUNY Poly Student Space is open at the Albany site.  After years of emails regarding the unprofessional nature of sitting in halls, undergraduate students finally have a place to study and relax in between classes.

Student Activities Hosts Laser Tag Event for SUNY Poly Students

The laser tag event at Zero Gravity Laser Tag was a huge success for the SUNY Poly Albany site Student Life in a multitude of ways; it was a great way to encourage and promote the mingling of different classes and reached an impressively large number of SUNY POLY students.

About SUNY Polytechnic Institutes' College of Arts and Sciences

The dearth of information regarding the College of Arts and Sciences and its courses has left the student body relatively unaware of its existence.  With no online course descriptions, the faculty distributed paper copies and sent out emails to the student body with information on these classes.  Due to the lack of student awareness, course enrollment has thus far been low.
