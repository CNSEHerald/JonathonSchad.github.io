Just Who is Merrick Garland?

 

 

By Leonardo Bezerra | 1 April 2016

World News

President Barack Obama nominated Merrick Garland to replace Justice Antonin Scalia

WLTX 19

With the recent death of Supreme Court Justice Antonin Scalia, there is now a hole within the court that must be filled.  President Barack Obama has nominated Merrick Garland, Chief Justice of the DC Circuit Court of Appeals, to fill that role.  If he were to be accepted by the senate, what sort of court official would he become?

 

Garland graduated from Harvard Law School with high honors. He worked as a clerk for former Justice William Brennan, and afterward, spent a few years working for law firm Arnold & Palmer.  He returned to working for the US government as an attorney, rising in the ranks from Deputy Assistant Attorney General to his current position as Chief Justice of the DC Circuit Court - arguably the second highest court in the United States.

 

Garland has overseen a number of high profile cases such as District of Columbia v. Heller, a ruling challenging DC’s gun control measures, and the prosecution of the Unabomber, Ted Kaczynski, in the Oklahoma City bombing.

 

If Garland were to be confirmed, he would be the oldest Supreme Court nominee since President Richard Nixon nominated Lewis Powell in 1971.  He has been nominated twice for the Supreme Court, once in 2009 and again in 2010.  He was passed over by Justices Sonia Sotomayor and Elena Kagan, respectively.  These two Justices replaced former Justices David Souter and John Paul Stevens, who were both considered liberal.

 

Garland can best be described as strongly centered on the political scale, with a slight tilt towards the left. His opinions are concise and avoid sweeping pronouncements. In recent press events, Garland stated “fidelity to the Constitution and the law have been the cornerstone of my professional life” and if confirmed, he would “continue on that course”.  Based on his previous rulings, Garland has been noted to be strongly pro-business, often voting against the EPA in favor of business interests, and has a tendency to be unsympathetic to the rights of criminal defendants.

 

Garland’s positions on social issues are largely unknown. Within the appeals court, he has shown deference toward agency decision makers. Since the DC Court of Appeals does not typically address cases dealing with social issues, his track record on broader topics such as gun control, abortion, and LGBT rights is effectively nonexistent.

 

Many experts speculate this is why he was selected. Republicans cannot point to a specific ruling that is counter to their values. Obama, indeed, may have selected Garland based on the bipartisan praise he has received in the past.

 

Garland has shown himself to be conscientious of his position as an unbiased judge. He has put aside personal views in order to give decisions that would be best for the nation, deferred to the courts above him, and called in additional judges for ‘en banc’ trials if a case is particularly significant.  En banc trials use a panel of judges, instead of one judge, to make a decision.

 

Should the GOP allow Garland’s nomination, the Supreme Court may see a shift to the left. Even if his own views may not be liberal, his votes could help to swing the deadlocked 4-4 on hot-button issues into a 5-4 victory for the Democrats.
