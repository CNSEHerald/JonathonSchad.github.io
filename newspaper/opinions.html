Professor's Spotlight

Dr. Susan Sharfstein

To many younger SUNY Poly students, Dr. Susan Sharfstein is the Chemistry professor.  Teaching introductory chemistry has a way of ‘typecasting’ a professor to younger students.

 

As students continue at CNSE, they will see more of Professor Sharfstein.  If a student is in the engineering track, they will also have her for ‘Fluid Mechanics and Transport Processes’, an extremely engineering heavy course.  Students who take the more biology geared classes will find Professor Sharfstein giving guest lectures on topics ranging from biologic batteries to biomanufacturing.

 

So what type of background must Professor Sharfstein have in order to teach chemistry, fluid dynamics, and all manner of biology?

Op-ed: Don't Do Drugs

“I think the public doesn’t fully appreciate yet the scope of the problem,” President Obama stated at the National Rx Drug Abuse & Heroin Summit.  The problem in need of discussion is the ubiquitous misuse and abuse of opioids, prescribed and illicit.  While many college students may consider using drugs as an interesting experience, or even a joke, the danger that these drugs pose is more serious than most realize.

Op-ed: Beyond the Buzzwords of New York's Finest Work-in-Progress

If buzzwords were flowers, then CNSE is a prolific botanical garden, replete with daffodils, tulips, and daisies.  In other words, our college, the 80% industry and 20% academia establishment, is surrounded by words like “world-class”, “leading-edge”, and “ground-breaking.”  The official website, along with news releases, is filled with these buzzwords.  But do they reflect the true nature of Dr. Kaloyeros’ brainchild for undergraduates?  Or are there more accurate ways to describe it?
