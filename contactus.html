Contact The Herald

Want to write for The Herald?

Email thecnseherald@gmail.com. Writing for the Herald is an open commitment with no experience necessary. You can write for us once, or many times. In your message, please include which sections you are interested in writing for and you can be added to our writers email list! This includes anyone interested in submitting art or writing for our advice column.

Have any suggestions or story ideas?

Email thecnseherald@gmail.com. If you have a story you want us to look into and publish or have any general suggestions for future content, please feel free to contact us. We will do everything in our power to accommodate any suggestions. Your input is important to us!

Need Advice?

Email thecnseherald@gmail.com. The CNSE Herald would like to include a advice series for those seeking advice, related to the university and otherwise. Feel free to contact us and we would be more than happy to answer your question in our next week's publication. For the sake of discretion, you may ask to have your name omitted or use an alias for the purpose of publishing. Unfortunately, submissions cannot be anonymous due to concerns of spam.

Interested in Web Design?

Email our resident web developer at jelwood@albany.edu . The Herald currently employs its Editor-in-Chief as a web designer with absolutely no experience. If you wish to gain experience in web design or love to do it, we would be thrilled to take you on board The Herald staff. No prior experience necessary.

Letters to the Editor

E-mail jelwood@albany.edu. Signed letters only. Students and alums, please include class year for publication. Non-students, please include city or University affiliation for publication. All: please include evening telephone number (not for publication).


Word limit for letters: 500.

Op-eds

E-mail thecnseherald@gmail.com. Students and alums, please include class year for publication. Non-students, please include city or University affiliation for publication. All: please include evening telephone number (not for publication).

Word limit for op-eds: 700.
